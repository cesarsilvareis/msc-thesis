1. Which of the following is NOT a common causative agent of infective endocarditis?
   a) Streptococcus viridans
   b) Staphylococcus aureus
   c) Pseudomonas aeruginosa
   d) Enterococcus faecalis

   Correct answer: c) Pseudomonas aeruginosa

2. What is the most common cardiac valve affected by infective endocarditis?
   a) Aortic valve
   b) Pulmonary valve
   c) Mitral valve
   d) Tricuspid valve

   Correct answer: a) Aortic valve

3. Which of the following is a classic clinical manifestation of infective endocarditis?
   a) Hemoptysis
   b) Janeway lesions
   c) Ascites
   d) Bone pain

   Correct answer: b) Janeway lesions

4. Which diagnostic test is most commonly used to confirm the presence of infective endocarditis?
   a) Chest X-ray
   b) Electrocardiogram (ECG)
   c) Blood culture
   d) Urine analysis

   Correct answer: c) Blood culture

5. What is the primary goal of antimicrobial therapy in treating infective endocarditis?
   a) Eradicate the causative pathogen
   b) Reduce fever and pain
   c) Prevent recurrence of endocarditis
   d) Repair damaged heart valves

   Correct answer: a) Eradicate the causative pathogen
