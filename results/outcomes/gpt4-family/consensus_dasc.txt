### Question:
What are the rare causes of blood culture negative IE? Which diagnostic procedures are adequate for non-bacterial pathogens?

### Table:

| **Cause** | **Description** | **Diagnostic Procedures** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Fastidious bacteria** | Includes organisms like HACEK group bacteria, Gemella, Granulicatella, Abiotrophia spp., and Propionibacterium acnes. | Prolonged incubation, special culture conditions, and PCR on excised valve tissue. |
| **Intracellular bacteria** | Common agents include Coxiella burnetii, Bartonella spp., and Tropheryma whipplei. | Serological tests for Coxiella and Bartonella; PCR for Tropheryma whipplei. |
| **Fungi** | Rarely causes IE, but Candida spp. is notable. | Culture and PCR on valve tissue. |
| **Non-infective causes** | Includes marantic endocarditis and endocarditis related to systemic diseases like lupus and Behçet’s disease. | Histological analysis and serology for autoimmune markers. |
| **Other rare pathogens** | Includes organisms like Mycobacterium, Legionella, Brucella, and Mycoplasma. | Culture, PCR, and specific serological tests. |
| **Non-bacterial thrombotic endocarditis** | Often associated with malignancies and autoimmune disorders. | Histological analysis of excised valve tissue. |
