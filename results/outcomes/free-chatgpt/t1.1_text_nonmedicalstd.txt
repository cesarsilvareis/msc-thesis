Infective Endocarditis is a serious infection of the inner lining of the heart chambers and valves, typically caused by bacteria entering the bloodstream. This condition often occurs when bacteria from another part of the body, like the mouth or skin, spread through the bloodstream and attach to damaged areas in the heart. Symptoms may include fever, fatigue, chills, and heart murmurs. If left untreated, it can lead to complications such as heart valve damage or even life-threatening conditions. Prompt diagnosis and treatment with antibiotics and sometimes surgery are crucial for managing Infective Endocarditis.
