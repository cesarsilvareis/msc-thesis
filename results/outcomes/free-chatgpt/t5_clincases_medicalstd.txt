Prompt 1: Implicit Evaluation Setup
**Clinical Case Study Evaluation Scheme**

**Case Scenario:**

A 45-year-old male patient presents to the emergency department with fever, chills, and malaise for the past week. He has a history of intravenous drug use and a recent dental procedure. On examination, you note a new-onset murmur and splinter hemorrhages on his fingernails.

**Instructions to Students:**

Please evaluate the following clinical case and answer the questions provided to demonstrate your understanding of Infective Endocarditis.

1\. **Identification and Diagnosis:**

\- Identify the potential risk factors for infective endocarditis in this patient.

\- Describe the clinical manifestations of infective endocarditis evident in this case.

\- Explain the diagnostic criteria you would employ to confirm the diagnosis of infective endocarditis in this patient.

2\. **Pathophysiology and Microbiology:**

\- Discuss the pathophysiological processes underlying the development of infective endocarditis.

\- Describe the microbial agents most commonly associated with infective endocarditis and their mechanisms of pathogenesis.

3\. **Complications and Prognosis:**

\- List the potential complications of infective endocarditis.

\- Explain the prognostic factors that may influence the outcome of infective endocarditis in this patient.

4\. **Management and Treatment:**

\- Outline the general principles of management for infective endocarditis.

\- Propose an appropriate antibiotic regimen for this patient based on the suspected causative organism and risk factors.

5\. **Prevention and Follow-Up:**

\- Discuss strategies for the prevention of infective endocarditis in high-risk individuals.

\- Outline the necessary follow-up and monitoring for patients following treatment for infective endocarditis.

**Additional Notes:**

\- Utilize your knowledge of microbiology, cardiology, infectious diseases, and clinical reasoning to answer the questions thoroughly.

\- Justify your answers with evidence-based medicine and clinical guidelines where applicable.

\- Consider the patient's history, clinical presentation, and potential complications when formulating your responses.

\- You have 30 minutes to complete the evaluation.
