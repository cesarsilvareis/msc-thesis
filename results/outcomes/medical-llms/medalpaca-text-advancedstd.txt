Infective Endocarditis is a kind of bacterial infection that occurs when bacteria or other microorganisms enter the bloodstream. If the bloodstream carries the bacteria to the heart, the bacteria may attach to damaged heart valves. The bacteria can grow and form infective vegetations on the valves. The infective vegetations may break off and travel to other parts of the body through the bloodstream, causing a condition known as bacterial septicemia. Infective Endocarditis is a serious condition that requires prompt medical attention.
